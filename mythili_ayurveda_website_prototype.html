Joint & Bone

Arthritis & Joint Pain

Known as Amavata (Rheumatoid) and Sandhivata (Osteoarthritis) in classical Ayurvedic texts — a condition rooted in impaired Agni and accumulated Ama (toxins) in the joints.

Understanding Arthritis in Ayurveda

In Ayurveda, arthritis is primarily a Vata disorder aggravated by accumulation of Ama (undigested metabolic waste). When Vata and Ama combine, they travel through channels (Srotas) and lodge in joints, causing inflammation, pain, and stiffness. Treatment must address both Ama elimination and Vata pacification simultaneously.

Symptoms we address

  • Morning stiffness lasting more than 30 minutes
  • Swelling, warmth, and redness in joints
  • Bilateral joint involvement (knuckles, knees, wrists)
  • Fatigue and generalised body pain
  • Loss of range of motion and grip strength
  • Low-grade fever in active inflammatory phases

Ayurvedic Treatment Protocol

1

Deepana-Pachana (2 weeks)

Kindling digestive fire and digesting accumulated Ama using Trikatu, Chitrakadi Vati, and warm herbal decoctions

2

Snehapana — Internal Oleation (5–7 days)

Medicated ghee (Guggulutiktaka Ghrita) administered internally to lubricate and prepare the channels for Panchakarma

3

Virechana — Therapeutic Purgation (1 day)

Elimination of Pitta-based toxins through controlled therapeutic purgation, reducing systemic inflammation

4

Vasti — Medicated Enema (8–30 days)

Kshiravasti and Mustadi Yapana Vasti to directly address Vata in the colon — the root seat of Vata disorders

5

Herbal Maintenance (3–6 months)

Rasayana therapy with Ashwagandha, Guggul, Shallaki, and Boswellia for long-term joint rebuilding

AshwagandhaShallakiGuggulNirgundiErandaPunarnavaRasnaDhanvantaram Taila

Psoriasis & Skin Disorders

Classified as Ekakushtha in Ayurveda — a chronic Kushtha (skin disease) involving vitiation of all three doshas, with Vata and Kapha predominance. Completely treatable with sustained Panchakarma and Rasayana.

Ayurvedic perspective

Ayurveda views psoriasis as a deep-seated Ama accumulation in Rasa and Rakta Dhatu (plasma and blood tissue). The abnormal skin cell turnover is a manifestation of impaired liver function, disturbed immunity, and blocked Srotas. Treatment must be systemic, not topical.

Treatment protocol

1

Snehana — Oleation

External and internal oleation with Mahatiktaka Ghrita to mobilise toxins from deep tissues

2

Virechana — Purgation

Deep cleansing of Rakta Dhatu through therapeutic purgation, addressing the root of skin inflammation

3

Takradhara — Medicated Buttermilk Therapy

Daily pouring of medicated buttermilk infused with Amalaki and Nimba over the scalp and body — a speciality treatment for psoriasis

4

Rasayana — Blood Purification

Manjistha, Neem, Khadira, and Sariva-based formulations for sustained blood purification over 3–6 months
